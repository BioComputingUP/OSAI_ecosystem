About OSAI

Building trust in life science AI: Reusable, Reproducible, Sustainable

Our Mission

To build a foundation of trust and accelerate scientific discovery by supporting researchers to leverage reusable, reproducible, and environmentally sustainable AI in the life sciences.

Our Story: From a Community Need to a Global Support Framework

Driven by the growing crisis in scientific AI, where models were becoming difficult to reuse, reproduce, and environmentally unsustainable, experts from across ELIXIR Europe came together to address these systemic challenges.

ELIXIR Machine Learning Focus Group

In a collaborative effort, over 30 specialists from the ELIXIR Machine Learning (ML) Focus Group worked to address the entire lifecycle of scientific AI. Their focus on the core challenges of Reusability, Reproducibility, and Environmental Sustainability culminated in the creation of the OSAI framework, a modern set of guidelines for responsible AI research.

OSAI Recommendations

The OSAI Recommendations provide a practical and holistic framework for responsible AI development in the life sciences. Presented in a key arXiv preprint, these modern guidelines help researchers build AI systems that are inherently reusable, reproducible, and environmentally sustainable.

Our Family of Solutions

To provide comprehensive support for researchers, the OSAI framework is complemented by a suite of specialised tools and foundational guidelines.

DOME Recommendations

The DOME Recommendations provide comprehensive guidelines for machine learning validation in the life sciences. Published in Nature Methods, these evidence-based standards help researchers document and validate their ML methodologies to ensure transparency, reproducibility, and scientific rigor.

DOME Registry

To translate these recommendations into practice, the DOME Registry was launched in 2023. This service provides a technical solution to help authors, reviewers, and publishers ensure that machine learning models are transparent, trustworthy, and truly reproducible. The Registry is under active development to support wider adoption across the scientific community.
